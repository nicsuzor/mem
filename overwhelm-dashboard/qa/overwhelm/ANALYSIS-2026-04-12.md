# Overwhelm Dashboard — Visual Audit (2026-04-12)

Model: gemini-3.1-pro-preview
Branch: feat/overwhelm-dashboard-improvements
Commit: 22187

## Executive summary
The Overwhelm Dashboard exhibits a strong, cohesive dark-mode aesthetic ("Operator System v1.0") that relies heavily on a terminal-inspired, data-dense look. The foundational elements—color palettes, typography (monospace fonts), and layout structures—are largely successful in conveying complex hierarchical and networked data. 

However, across the various visualizations (Treemap, Circle Pack, Force, Metro, Arc), a consistent theme of text legibility and label collision issues emerges. While the macro-structures look impressive, the micro-interactions and readability at lower zoom levels suffer. Dense regions lead to unreadable text, and layout algorithms sometimes fail to optimize space or prevent overlaps. Addressing these legibility and layout efficiency issues will significantly elevate the usability of the tool.

## Per-view findings

### 01 — Dashboard
[screenshot reference: 01-dashboard.png]
**Works:** 
- Strong visual hierarchy with distinct sections for Current Activity, Today's Story, and Dropped Threads.
- Status indicators (running, paused) are clear and use color effectively.
- Excellent use of contrast in the dark theme.
**Broken/confusing:** 
- "Dropped Threads" list contains repetitive error messages and visual noise.
- Some list items have alignment issues with their badges/tags.
**Improvements:**
- [easy] Deduplicate or group similar error messages in "Dropped Threads".
- [medium] Improve horizontal alignment of badges and timestamps in the activity lists.

### 02 — Treemap
[screenshot reference: 02-treemap.png]
**Works:** 
- Excellent at conveying the relative weight/priority of different epics and tasks.
- Color coding effectively groups related project areas.
**Broken/confusing:** 
- Severe text clipping and illegibility on smaller rectangles.
- Text often overflows the bounds of its container without proper ellipsis.
**Improvements:**
- [easy] Add CSS `text-overflow: ellipsis` and `overflow: hidden` to node labels.
- [medium] Implement dynamic font sizing or hide text entirely on nodes below a certain dimension threshold until hovered/zoomed.

### 03 — Circle Pack
[screenshot reference: 03-circle-pack.png]
**Works:** 
- Clearly visualizes the depth of hierarchies (epics > projects > tasks).
- The circular containment metaphor is intuitive.
**Broken/confusing:** 
- Significant amounts of wasted space between packed circles.
- Labels overlap heavily in dense clusters and often cross circle boundaries.
**Improvements:**
- [medium] Adjust the d3 pack padding to be tighter, maximizing screen real estate.
- [hard] Implement a label collision detection step to hide or offset labels that overlap.

### 04 — Force Directed
[screenshot reference: 04-force-directed.png]
**Works:** 
- Shows the organic structure and clustering of the knowledge graph.
**Broken/confusing:** 
- Default zoom level is too far out, rendering all text microscopic.
- The graph is overly sparse with long, stretching edges and disconnected components floating far away.
- Nodes overlap links and each other.
**Improvements:**
- [easy] Adjust default camera bounds to zoom in on the primary dense cluster.
- [medium] Tune the force layout simulation parameters (gravity, link distance, charge) to create a more compact and readable graph.

### 05 — Metro
[screenshot reference: 05-metro.png]
**Works:** 
- Visually striking; the subway-map aesthetic clearly communicates paths and sequences.
- Distinct line colors help trace dependencies across the graph.
**Broken/confusing:** 
- Node labels frequently overlap the "tracks" (edges), making both hard to read.
- Complex intersections result in visually messy routing.
**Improvements:**
- [medium] Add a slight dark stroke/outline (halo) to text labels so they remain readable when drawn over brightly colored edges.
- [hard] Improve the edge routing algorithm to reduce crossings and maintain parallel tracks more cleanly.

### 06 — Arc Diagram
[screenshot reference: 06-arc-diagram.png]
**Works:** 
- Effectively flattens the graph to show chronological or sequential flow.
- Highlights the volume of cross-linking.
**Broken/confusing:** 
- Vertical labels on the x-axis are very difficult to read at a glance.
- Nodes are extremely small, making hover targets difficult to hit.
**Improvements:**
- [easy] Increase the base radius of the nodes for better interaction.
- [medium] Angle the labels at 45 degrees instead of 90 degrees to improve readability.

### 07 — Threaded Tasks
[screenshot reference: 07-threaded-tasks.png]
**Works:** 
- Very clean, readable list view.
- Tag and status badge alignment is consistent and scannable.
**Broken/confusing:** 
- Lots of empty horizontal space; the view feels a bit sparse on wide screens.
**Improvements:**
- [easy] Introduce a max-width to the container to keep line lengths comfortable for reading.
- [medium] Utilize the empty right-hand space for additional metadata columns (e.g., tags, due dates, dependencies).

### 08 — Config Overlay
[screenshot reference: 08-config-overlay.png]
**Works:** 
- Overlays (like the Treemap Config and Visibility filters) are well-designed and don't obscure the main view completely.
- Toggle switches and radio buttons are clear.
**Broken/confusing:** 
- Multiple panels open simultaneously can create UI clutter.
**Improvements:**
- [easy] Add an auto-close or mutually exclusive toggle behavior so opening one config panel closes the others.
